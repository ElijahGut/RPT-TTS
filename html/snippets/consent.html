Perception of Spoken Discourse Meaning

Online Consent

You are invited to participate in a research study on how speakers perceive prominence and boundaries in English. The purpose of the study is to advance scientific knowledge about how people use language. This study is conducted by Jennifer Cole of the Linguistics Department from the University of Illinois Urbana Champaign.

This study will take approximately 5 minutes of your time. You will be asked to annotate the transcripts for a set of associated sound files.

Your decision to participate or decline participation in this study is completely voluntary and you have the right to terminate your participation at any time without penalty. If you want do not wish to complete this survey just close your browser.

If you are affiliated with the University of Illinois in any capacity, your decision to participate, decline, or withdraw from participation will have no effect on your current status or future relations with the University of Illinois.

Your participation in this research will be completely confidential and data will be averaged and reported in aggregate. Possible outlets of dissemination may be academic conferences and journal publications. Although your participation in this research may not benefit you personally, it will help us understand aspects of language perception.

There are no risks to individuals participating in this survey beyond those that exist in daily life
